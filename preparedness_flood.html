Preparedness

Flood

A flood is an overflow of water that submerges land which is usually dry. Flooding may occur as an overflow of water from water bodies, such as a river or lake, in which the water overtops or breaks levees, resulting in some of that water escaping its usual boundaries, or it may occur due to an accumulation of rainwater on saturated ground in an a real flood. Some floods develop slowly, while others known as ‘Flash Floods’ can occur within minutes or hours. Flash Floods normally have a dangerous wall of roaring water carrying rocks, mud, and other debris.

Always be vigilant of flooding, the risk factor isn’t just based on history, it’s based on a huge number of factors: topography, control measures, tidal data, river data, and changes to new construction and development. Avoid building or buying in a known flood risk area; unless you elevate and reinforce your home. Look into installing ‘check valves’ to prevent flood water from backing up into the drains in your home. If appropriate, construct barriers to stop floodwater from entering your home/building and seal walls in basements with waterproofing compounds. Always be prepared to quickly evacuate an area if so instructed by the authorities. Be very careful to understand your plan and do not drive towards flooded areas. Six inches of water will reach the bottom of most cars and cause loss of control and stalling of engines. One foot of water will float most vehicles and two feet will carry away most cars including SUVs and trucks. Do not attempt to drive through standing water. There is no way to know the depth and the road way may be washed out underneath that water. You could be stranded, trapped or even washed away. Do not drive around barricades, they have been put there for a reason. Do not take short cuts, stick to designated evacuation routs. Be especially careful at night as it is much harder to recognized flood dangers.

Stay tuned to the radio and television for information. Stay on constant guard for flash flooding; if there is any danger or possibility of a flash flood, move immediately to higher ground; do not wait for instructions. Be aware of streams, canals, drainage canals as they tend to flood very quickly. If you have time to prepare to evacuate your home then secure your home by moving essentials items to an upper level. If you have time bring in outdoor furniture. Turn off all utilities at the main connection. Unplug all electrical appliances but do not do this if you are in standing water. Do not walk or drive through moving water. Six inches of water will knock you off your feet and sweep you away. Do not camp or park near a stream, river, creek, or canal.

Stay tuned to local news to secure alerts and warning system broadcasts to receive information, you never know it’s over until it’s over. Floods can come in spurts. Avoid moving water; stay away from damaged areas. If you are asked for help, give it; if not, stay away, don’t be a sightseer. Return to home only with specific authorization from the authorities. You don’t want to get in trouble with them; simply because you didn’t ask. Floods can change familiar roads and landscapes in a hurry. Roads that you travel daily could be washed away or ready to erode with the next car’s passing. Be very careful and always study the road far enough ahead to quickly stop and change direction. Be sure to have on heavy footwear when walking in flood waters as there may be hidden broken bottles, dead animals, or live snakes underneath the water. Stay out of buildings if it is surrounded by floodwater. Use extreme caution when entering buildings; there may be hidden damage particularly in foundations. Avoid exposure to floodwaters as it may be contaminated by gasoline, oil, or raw sewage. Service damaged septic systems quickly; as they can lead to serious health problems. Clean and disinfect everything that got wet. Be sure to have a clean, clear bill of health before drinking the community water. Be sure to have a clear understanding that it is safe before you turn your utilities back on and never do it alone.
